sushi su·shi noun \'sü-shē\

cold rice dressed with vinegar formed into any of various shapes and garnished
especially with bits of raw seafood or vegetables [Merrian Webster]

a food of Japanese origin consisting of cooked vinegared rice (shari) combined with other ingredients (neta) [Wikipedia]

It's All About the Fish Rice

Looking at these two (of many) definitions of sushi, if nothing else we gather that sushi is, first and foremost,
about the rice, not raw fish, as many Westerners assume. That's not to say that their isn't a fair amount of
sushi containing raw fish. Sushi chefs will tell you that it takes a lifetime to perfect the art of sushi-making,
because it takes a lifetime to perfect the preperation of sushi rice.
